**GOOD MANUFACTURING PRACTICES (GMP) – WHO MAIN PRINCIPLES (READY TEXT
BLOCK)**
---
### 1. QUALITY MANAGEMENT
The manufacturer must establish a comprehensive Quality Management System
(QMS) that ensures products are consistently produced and controlled
according to quality standards appropriate for their intended use.
Key requirements:
* Clearly defined quality policy and objectives
* Quality assurance (QA) and quality control (QC) systems
* Risk management principles applied throughout lifecycle
* Documentation of all processes
* Regular internal audits (self-inspection)
* Corrective and Preventive Actions (CAPA)
---
### 2. SANITATION AND HYGIENE
A high level of sanitation and hygiene must be practiced in all aspects of
manufacturing.
Requirements:
* Personnel must maintain personal cleanliness
* Appropriate protective clothing must be worn
* No eating, drinking, or smoking in production areas
* Premises must be cleaned regularly
* Written sanitation procedures must be followed
---
### 3. QUALIFICATION AND VALIDATION
Processes and systems must be validated to ensure consistent performance.
Includes:
* Equipment qualification (IQ, OQ, PQ)
* Process validation
* Cleaning validation
* Analytical method validation
* Periodic revalidation
---
### 4. COMPLAINTS AND PRODUCT RECALL
There must be a system to handle complaints and recall defective products.
Requirements:
* All complaints must be recorded and investigated
* Root cause analysis must be performed
* Effective recall system must exist
* Recall procedures must be tested regularly
---

--- PAGE 2 ---

### 5. CONTRACT PRODUCTION AND ANALYSIS
When manufacturing or testing is outsourced:
* Written contract must define responsibilities
* Contract acceptor must comply with GMP
* Contract giver must evaluate contractor competence
* No unauthorized subcontracting allowed
---
### 6. SELF-INSPECTION AND QUALITY AUDITS
Regular self-inspections must be conducted to ensure compliance.
Requirements:
* Independent internal audit team
* Documentation of findings
* Follow-up on corrective actions
---
### 7. PERSONNEL
Personnel must be qualified and trained.
Requirements:
* Clear organizational structure
* Defined responsibilities
* Initial and ongoing training programs
* Medical examination where required
Key roles:
* Head of Production
* Head of Quality Control
* Qualified Person
---
### 8. PREMISES
Premises must be designed to minimize risk of errors and contamination.
Requirements:
* Logical layout of production areas
* Adequate space for operations
* Controlled environmental conditions (temperature, humidity)
* Separate areas for different operations
* Pest control systems
---
### 9. EQUIPMENT
Equipment must be appropriate and properly maintained.
Requirements:
* Designed to prevent contamination
* Easy to clean and maintain

--- PAGE 3 ---

* Regular calibration and maintenance
* Equipment logs must be maintained
---
### 10. MATERIALS
Materials must be handled properly from receipt to use.
Requirements:
* Approved suppliers only
* Proper labeling and storage
* Quarantine system before approval
* FIFO (First In First Out) / FEFO (First Expiry First Out)
---
### 11. DOCUMENTATION
Documentation is critical to GMP compliance.
Requirements:
* Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
* Batch Manufacturing Records (BMR)
* Batch Packaging Records (BPR)
* Logbooks and reports
Principles:
* Data integrity (ALCOA: Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original,
Accurate)
* No overwriting; corrections must be signed and dated
---
### 12. GOOD PRACTICES IN PRODUCTION
Production must follow clearly defined procedures.
Requirements:
* Prevention of cross-contamination
* Line clearance before production
* In-process controls
* Proper labeling and identification
---
### 13. GOOD PRACTICES IN QUALITY CONTROL
Quality Control ensures products meet specifications.
Requirements:
* Sampling procedures
* Testing of raw materials, intermediates, and finished products
* Stability testing
* Retention samples must be stored
---
### 14. CONTRACT LABORATORIES

--- PAGE 4 ---

External labs must:
* Follow GMP principles
* Be validated and approved
* Maintain proper documentation
---
### 15. DISTRIBUTION AND STORAGE
Products must be stored and distributed properly.
Requirements:
* Controlled storage conditions
* Traceability of distribution
* Prevention of mix-ups
* Proper transportation conditions
---
### 16. PRODUCT QUALITY REVIEW
Periodic review of products must be conducted.
Includes:
* Review of deviations
* Complaints and recalls
* Stability data
* Process consistency
---
### 17. RISK MANAGEMENT
Risk management must be applied across all operations.
Steps:
* Risk identification
* Risk analysis
* Risk control
* Risk review
---
### 18. DATA INTEGRITY
All data must be:
* Accurate
* Complete
* Secure
* Traceable
Systems must prevent:
* Data manipulation
* Unauthorized access
---

**FDA cGMP (ICH Q7) – GOOD MANUFACTURING PRACTICE FOR ACTIVE PHARMACEUTICAL
INGREDIENTS (READY TEXT BLOCK)**
---
### 1. QUALITY MANAGEMENT
A comprehensive quality management system must be established to ensure APIs
meet required quality standards.
Requirements:
* Defined quality policy and responsibilities
* Quality unit independent of production
* Approval/rejection of materials and products by quality unit
* Change control system for any modification
* Deviation management and investigation
* Corrective and Preventive Actions (CAPA)
---
### 2. PERSONNEL
Personnel must be qualified, trained, and competent.
Requirements:
* Adequate number of qualified staff
* Training in GMP and job-specific operations
* Ongoing training programs
* Personnel hygiene practices must be maintained
* Consultants must be qualified and documented
---
### 3. BUILDINGS AND FACILITIES
Facilities must be suitable for intended operations.
Requirements:
* Adequate space and logical flow of materials
* Controlled environmental conditions
* Proper ventilation, filtration, and air handling systems
* Segregation to prevent contamination and mix-ups
* Adequate lighting and sanitation facilities
---
### 4. PROCESS EQUIPMENT
Equipment must be properly designed and maintained.
Requirements:
* Equipment must be suitable for intended use
* Cleaning procedures must be validated
* Calibration must be performed regularly
* Equipment logs must be maintained
* Computerized systems must be validated

--- PAGE 6 ---

---
### 5. DOCUMENTATION AND RECORDS
All activities must be documented.
Requirements:
* Written procedures (SOPs) must exist
* Batch production records must be maintained
* Equipment cleaning and use logs required
* Records must be retained for defined periods
* Entries must be accurate, legible, and traceable
Data integrity principles:
* ALCOA (Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate)
---
### 6. MATERIALS MANAGEMENT
All materials must be properly handled and controlled.
Requirements:
* Approved suppliers only
* Incoming materials must be tested or qualified
* Proper labeling and identification
* Quarantine before release
* Storage under appropriate conditions
* Use of FIFO/FEFO principles
---
### 7. PRODUCTION AND IN-PROCESS CONTROLS
Production must follow approved procedures.
Requirements:
* Written production instructions
* In-process controls to monitor quality
* Prevention of cross-contamination
* Time limits for processing stages
* Yield calculations and reconciliation
* Reprocessing/reworking must be justified and documented
---
### 8. PACKAGING AND LABELING
Packaging and labeling must ensure product identity and traceability.
Requirements:
* Correct labeling materials must be used
* Label issuance must be controlled
* Packaging operations must prevent mix-ups
* Printed materials must be verified
* Expiry date and batch number must be clear
---

--- PAGE 7 ---

### 9. STORAGE AND DISTRIBUTION
APIs must be stored and distributed under controlled conditions.
Requirements:
* Storage conditions must maintain product quality
* Distribution records must ensure traceability
* Transportation must prevent contamination and damage
* Returned materials must be evaluated
---
### 10. LABORATORY CONTROLS
Laboratory testing ensures product compliance.
Requirements:
* Specifications must be defined for materials and products
* Validated analytical methods must be used
* Stability testing programs required
* Retention samples must be stored
* Out-of-specification (OOS) results must be investigated
---
### 11. VALIDATION
Critical processes must be validated.
Requirements:
* Process validation to ensure consistency
* Cleaning validation
* Analytical method validation
* Computer system validation
* Periodic review and revalidation
---
### 12. CHANGE CONTROL
All changes must be controlled and documented.
Requirements:
* Formal change control system
* Impact assessment on product quality
* Approval before implementation
* Documentation of all changes
---
### 13. REJECTION AND REUSE OF MATERIALS
Non-conforming materials must be controlled.
Requirements:
* Rejected materials must be clearly identified
* Investigation must be conducted
* Reprocessing only if justified and approved
* Documentation required for reuse

--- PAGE 8 ---

---
### 14. COMPLAINTS AND RECALLS
Systems must exist to handle product issues.
Requirements:
* Complaint records must be maintained
* Investigations must determine root cause
* Recall procedures must be established
* Regulatory authorities must be informed when required
---
### 15. CONTRACT MANUFACTURERS AND LABORATORIES
Outsourced activities must be controlled.
Requirements:
* Written agreements defining responsibilities
* Contractor must comply with GMP
* Regular audits of contract facilities
* No unauthorized subcontracting
---
### 16. SELF-INSPECTION
Internal audits must ensure compliance.
Requirements:
* Periodic GMP audits
* Documentation of findings
* Corrective actions must be implemented
---
### 17. AGENTS, BROKERS, TRADERS, DISTRIBUTORS
Intermediaries must maintain quality standards.
Requirements:
* Proper documentation of transactions
* Traceability of materials
* Compliance with storage and transport requirements
---
### 18. SPECIFIC GUIDANCE FOR APIs
Additional controls for API manufacturing:
Requirements:
* Control of critical process steps
* Impurity profiling
* Stability monitoring
* Validation of sterilization processes (if applicable)

**PIC/S GMP GUIDE (PE 009 – PART I) – BASIC REQUIREMENTS FOR MEDICINAL
PRODUCTS (READY TEXT BLOCK)**
---
### 1. PHARMACEUTICAL QUALITY SYSTEM (PQS)
The manufacturer must establish and maintain a Pharmaceutical Quality System
that ensures products are consistently produced and controlled.
Requirements:
* Defined quality policy and objectives
* Management responsibility and oversight
* Continuous improvement system
* Quality risk management implementation
* Product lifecycle approach
* Change management system
* Deviation handling and CAPA system
---
### 2. PERSONNEL
Personnel must be sufficient in number and appropriately qualified.
Requirements:
* Clear organizational structure
* Defined roles and responsibilities
* Key personnel: Head of Production, Head of Quality Control, Qualified
Person (QP)
* Initial and ongoing GMP training
* Hygiene procedures must be followed
* Personnel must not be engaged in multiple conflicting roles
---
### 3. PREMISES AND EQUIPMENT
Premises and equipment must be designed to minimize risk.
#### Premises Requirements:
* Logical layout to prevent mix-ups and contamination
* Adequate space for operations and storage
* Controlled environmental conditions
* Dedicated areas for specific operations
* Pest control and sanitation systems
#### Equipment Requirements:
* Suitable design and capacity
* Easy cleaning and maintenance
* Calibration and qualification required
* Equipment status labeling (cleaned, in use, etc.)
* Maintenance logs must be maintained
---

--- PAGE 10 ---

### 4. DOCUMENTATION
Documentation must ensure traceability and accountability.
Requirements:
* Specifications for materials and products
* Manufacturing formulas and instructions
* Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
* Batch Processing Records (BPR)
* Batch Packaging Records
Principles:
* Data must follow ALCOA (Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original,
Accurate)
* No unauthorized changes
* Corrections must be signed and dated
---
### 5. PRODUCTION
Production must be carried out according to predefined procedures.
Requirements:
* Approved instructions must be followed
* Line clearance before operations
* Prevention of cross-contamination
* In-process controls must be performed
* Yield reconciliation required
* Handling of deviations during production
---
### 6. QUALITY CONTROL (QC)
Quality Control ensures compliance with specifications.
Requirements:
* Sampling and testing procedures
* Approval or rejection of materials and products
* Stability testing programs
* Retention samples must be stored
* Investigation of Out-of-Specification (OOS) results
---
### 7. OUTSOURCED ACTIVITIES
All outsourced activities must be controlled.
Requirements:
* Written contracts defining responsibilities
* Contract acceptor must comply with GMP
* Contract giver must audit contractor
* No subcontracting without approval
---

Systems must exist for handling complaints and recalls.
Requirements:
* Recording and investigation of complaints
* Identification of root causes
* Effective recall procedures
* Regular evaluation of recall effectiveness
---
### 9. SELF-INSPECTION
Regular internal audits must be conducted.
Requirements:
* Planned self-inspection program
* Independent auditors
* Documentation of findings
* Implementation of corrective actions
---
### 10. QUALITY RISK MANAGEMENT
Risk management must be applied to all processes.
Requirements:
* Risk identification, analysis, and evaluation
* Risk control and mitigation

* Use of scientific knowledge and experience
---
### 11. CHANGE CONTROL
Changes must be formally controlled.
Requirements:
* Formal change control procedures

* Documentation and review of changes

All deviations must be managed systematically.
Requirements:

**WHO-GMP CERTIFICATION GUIDELINES (INDIA) – READY TEXT BLOCK**
---
### 1. APPLICATION FOR WHO-GMP CERTIFICATE
Manufacturers must apply for WHO-GMP certification through the State
Licensing Authority (SLA) or CDSCO.
Requirements:
* Submission of prescribed application form
* Payment of applicable fees
* Details of manufacturing site and products
* Copy of valid manufacturing license
* Site Master File (SMF) submission
---
### 2. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
The applicant must:
* Hold a valid manufacturing license under Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940
* Comply with Schedule M (Indian GMP requirements)
* Manufacture products for export or intend to export
* Have adequate facilities and qualified personnel
---
### 3. SITE MASTER FILE (SMF)
A Site Master File must be submitted detailing:
* General information about manufacturer
* Quality management system
* Personnel details
* Premises and equipment
* Documentation system
* Production operations
* Quality control system
* Distribution and complaint handling
---

--- PAGE 14 ---

### 4. INSPECTION FOR CERTIFICATION
Inspection is conducted by CDSCO or State Drug Authority.
Requirements:
* Inspection of premises, equipment, and documentation
* Verification of GMP compliance (Schedule M / WHO GMP)
* Review of SOPs, batch records, validation reports
* Observation of manufacturing and QC processes
Outcome:
* Compliance report generated
* Deficiencies categorized (critical, major, minor)
---
### 5. DEFICIENCY MANAGEMENT
If deficiencies are observed:
* Manufacturer must submit corrective action plan (CAPA)
* Timeline for compliance must be defined
* Re-inspection may be required for major deficiencies
* Certification is granted only after compliance
---
### 6. GRANT OF WHO-GMP CERTIFICATE
Certificate is issued after successful inspection.
Conditions:
* Compliance with WHO GMP standards
* No critical deficiencies
* Adequate CAPA implementation
Certificate validity:
* Generally valid for 2–3 years (subject to authority rules)
---
### 7. PRODUCT-SPECIFIC CERTIFICATION
WHO-GMP certificate may include:
* List of approved products
* Dosage forms
* Manufacturing categories
Requirement:
* Only approved products can be exported under certificate
---
### 8. CONTINUOUS COMPLIANCE
Post-certification, manufacturer must:

--- PAGE 15 ---

* Maintain GMP compliance at all times
* Implement ongoing quality improvements
* Report major changes to authorities
* Allow periodic inspections
---
### 9. CHANGE CONTROL
Any significant changes must be reported:
Includes:
* Change in premises or equipment
* Change in manufacturing process
* Change in key personnel
* Change in product composition
Approval required before implementation in certain cases.
---
### 10. RENEWAL OF CERTIFICATE
Manufacturers must apply for renewal before expiry.
Requirements:
* Updated documentation
* Compliance history
* Previous inspection reports
* Continued adherence to GMP
---
### 11. SUSPENSION OR CANCELLATION
Certificate may be suspended or cancelled if:
* Non-compliance with GMP
* False or misleading information
* Failure to implement CAPA
* Serious quality defects or safety issues
---
### 12. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
Mandatory documents include:
* Site Master File (SMF)
* SOPs and quality manuals
* Batch Manufacturing Records (BMR)
* Validation reports
* Training records
* Equipment logs
* Complaint and recall records
---
### 13. EXPORT COMPLIANCE
For export purposes:

--- PAGE 16 ---

* WHO-GMP certificate required by importing countries
* Certificate of Pharmaceutical Product (COPP) may be issued
* Compliance with importing country regulations required
---
### 14. REGULATORY AUTHORITIES
Authorities involved:
* CDSCO (Central Drugs Standard Control Organization)
* State Licensing Authority (SLA)
Responsibilities:
* Inspection and certification
* Enforcement of GMP compliance
* Issuance of WHO-GMP certificates
---
### 15. GOOD DOCUMENTATION PRACTICES (GDP)
All records must follow:
* Accuracy and completeness
* Timely recording
* No overwriting or falsification
* Signed and dated entries
* Traceability of all activities
---
### 16. TRAINING AND PERSONNEL COMPLIANCE
Requirements:
* GMP training for all employees
* Periodic training updates
* Documentation of training records
* Qualified technical staff
---
### 17. QUALITY ASSURANCE RESPONSIBILITIES
QA must ensure:
* Compliance with GMP guidelines
* Approval of procedures and records
* Handling deviations and CAPA
* Release or rejection of batches
---
### 18. RECALL AND COMPLAINT HANDLING
Requirements:
* System for handling complaints
* Investigation of product defects
* Recall procedures must be in place
* Regulatory authorities must be informed if required
