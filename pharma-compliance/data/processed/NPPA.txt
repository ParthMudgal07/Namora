## 📘DRUGS (PRICES CONTROL) ORDER (DPCO), 2013
### Regulations, Guidelines & Compliance Requirements
### 1. Legal Basis
* Issued under the Essential Commodities Act, 1955
* Enforced by the National Pharmaceutical Pricing Authority (NPPA)
* Applicable across India
---
### 2. Scope of DPCO 2013
* Covers:
* Scheduled formulations (as per National List of Essential Medicines –
NLEM)
* Non-scheduled formulations
* Applies to:
* Manufacturers
* Importers
* Distributors
---
### 3. Price Control Regulations
#### 3.1 Ceiling Price Fixation (Para 4)
* NPPA fixes ceiling prices for scheduled formulations
* Based on market-based pricing (average of brands ≥1% market share)
* Includes retailer margin
* No manufacturer can sell above the ceiling price
---
#### 3.2 Retail Price of New Drugs (Para 5)
* NPPA fixes retail price for new drugs
* Mandatory approval required before launch
* Price must comply with prescribed formula
---
#### 3.3 Revision of Prices (Para 16)
* Prices must be revised as per NPPA notifications
* Manufacturers must implement revised prices within specified timelines
---
### 4. Compliance Requirements
#### 4.1 Price List Display (Para 24)
* Every manufacturer must:
* Issue a price list in prescribed format (Form V)
* Display it at business premises
* Provide copies to dealers and authorities
---

--- PAGE 2 ---

#### 4.2 Submission of Information (Para 21)
* Mandatory submission of:
* Production data
* Sales data
* Pricing details
* Must be provided to NPPA when required
---
#### 4.3 Maintenance of Records (Para 22)
* Companies must maintain:
* Detailed records of production and sales
* Pricing and distribution data
* Records must be available for inspection
---
### 5. Regulation of Non-Scheduled Drugs (Para 20)
* Manufacturers can increase price up to 10% annually
* Any increase beyond 10% triggers NPPA intervention
---
### 6. Overcharging Provisions (Para 13)
* If a company charges above ceiling price:
* Must deposit overcharged amount
* Along with interest
* Recovery is mandatory and enforceable
---
### 7. Discontinuation of Production (Para 21(2))
* Manufacturer must:
* Inform NPPA at least 6 months prior to discontinuation
* NPPA may direct continued production in public interest
---
### 8. Monitoring & Enforcement
#### 8.1 Power of NPPA
* Fix/revise drug prices
* Monitor availability
* Ensure compliance
---
#### 8.2 Inspection & Audit
* NPPA can:
* Inspect company premises
* Audit records

--- PAGE 3 ---

* Investigate violations
---
### 9. Penalties for Non-Compliance
* Recovery of overcharged amount
* Interest penalties
* Legal proceedings under Essential Commodities Act
* Possible prosecution
---
### 10. Key Compliance Checklist
* Do not exceed NPPA ceiling price
* Obtain approval for new drug pricing
* Update price lists regularly
* Submit required data to NPPA
* Maintain proper documentation
* Avoid unauthorized price increases
* Notify before discontinuation
---
### 11. Practical Guidelines for Industry
* Regularly track NPPA notifications
* Integrate pricing updates into ERP systems
* Conduct internal compliance audits
* Train regulatory and pricing teams
* Ensure transparency in pricing practices
---
### 12. Conclusion
DPCO 2013 establishes a structured framework to:
* Control drug prices
* Prevent overcharging
* Ensure availability of essential medicines
Strict compliance is mandatory for all pharmaceutical stakeholders operating
in India.
## 📘NATIONAL PHARMACEUTICAL PRICING POLICY (NPPP), 2012
### Regulations, Guidelines & Compliance Framework
### 1. Objective of NPPP 2012
* Ensure availability of essential medicines at affordable prices
* Regulate drug pricing through a transparent mechanism
* Balance public health concerns with pharmaceutical industry growth
---
### 2. Scope of Policy
* Applies to:

--- PAGE 4 ---

* All formulations listed in the National List of Essential Medicines
(NLEM)
* Covers:
* Pricing of essential drugs
* Monitoring of non-essential drugs
---
### 3. Pricing Policy Framework
#### 3.1 Market-Based Pricing (MBP)
* Ceiling price is based on:
* Simple average of prices of all brands having ≥1% market share
* Retail margin included in final price
* Replaces cost-based pricing system
---
#### 3.2 Scheduled Drugs (NLEM-Based Control)
* Prices of essential medicines are controlled
* NPPA fixes ceiling price
* All manufacturers must comply
---
#### 3.3 Non-Scheduled Drugs
* Prices are not directly fixed
* Annual price increase capped at 10%
* NPPA monitors abnormal increases
---
### 4. Role of NPPA under NPPP
* Fix ceiling prices of essential medicines
* Monitor price trends of non-scheduled drugs
* Ensure availability of medicines
* Enforce compliance with pricing norms
---
### 5. Data & Reporting Requirements
* Pharmaceutical companies must:
* Provide pricing data
* Submit market-based data when required
* Ensure transparency in pricing
---
### 6. Regulatory Principles
#### 6.1 Essentiality-Based Control
* Only essential medicines are subject to strict price control
* Based on National List of Essential Medicines (NLEM)
---

--- PAGE 5 ---

#### 6.2 Transparency
* Pricing methodology must be clear and justifiable
* Public access to price notifications
---
#### 6.3 Non-Discrimination
* Uniform pricing rules across manufacturers
* No preferential treatment
---
### 7. Price Monitoring Mechanism
* Continuous monitoring of:
* Drug prices
* Availability in the market
* Intervention in case of:
* Shortages
* Excessive price increases
---
### 8. Compliance Requirements for Industry
* Adhere to NPPA-fixed ceiling prices
* Maintain pricing transparency
* Avoid artificial inflation of prices
* Ensure continuous supply of essential medicines
* Submit required data when demanded
---
### 9. Enforcement Guidelines
* NPPA empowered to:
* Issue price notifications
* Monitor compliance
* Take corrective actions
---
### 10. Penalties & Corrective Actions
* Overcharging leads to:
* Recovery of excess amount
* Interest penalties
* Non-compliance may result in:
* Legal action
* Regulatory intervention
---
### 11. Key Policy Outcomes
* Shift from cost-based to market-based pricing

--- PAGE 6 ---

* Focus on affordability and accessibility
* Strengthened regulatory oversight
---
### 12. Practical Guidelines for Implementation
* Regularly track NLEM updates
* Align product pricing with NPPA notifications
* Maintain robust data systems for compliance
* Conduct periodic internal pricing audits
---
### 13. Conclusion
NPPP 2012 establishes a balanced framework that:
* Ensures access to essential medicines
* Promotes fair pricing practices
* Supports sustainable growth of the pharmaceutical sector
## 📘KEY PRICING REGULATIONS (NPPA / DPCO 2013)
### 1. Ceiling Price of Scheduled Formulations (Para 4)
* NPPA shall fix the ceiling price of scheduled formulations listed under
NLEM
* Ceiling price is calculated using market-based pricing methodology
* Formula:
* Simple average of price to retailer of all brands having ≥1% market share
* Retailer margin is included in the ceiling price
**Compliance Requirements:**
* No manufacturer shall sell above the notified ceiling price
* Prices must be revised immediately after NPPA notification
* All brands of the same formulation must comply uniformly
---
### 2. Retail Price Fixation of New Drugs (Para 5)
* NPPA shall fix retail price for:
* New drugs
* New formulations of existing drugs
**Regulatory Requirements:**
* Prior price approval is mandatory before market launch
* Pricing must follow prescribed calculation methodology
* Manufacturer cannot independently determine price
---
### 3. Calculation of Ceiling Price (Para 6)
* Based on:
* Average price of all brands with ≥1% market share
* Data source:

--- PAGE 7 ---

* Market-based data (e.g., pharmaceutical sales data)
**Guidelines:**
* Only relevant and valid market data shall be considered
* Outliers and non-representative data may be excluded
---
### 4. Retail Price Calculation Formula (Para 5 & 6)
* Retail Price (RP) is calculated as:
RP = (Cost + Margin) + Applicable Taxes
**Guidelines:**
* Must include retailer margin
* Taxes are applied separately as per law
---
### 5. Price Revision of Scheduled Drugs (Para 16)
* Annual price revision allowed based on:
* Wholesale Price Index (WPI)
**Compliance Requirements:**
* Manufacturers must:
* Adjust prices as per WPI notification
* Not exceed revised ceiling price
* Revision must be implemented within prescribed time
---
### 6. Pricing of Non-Scheduled Formulations (Para 20)
* Manufacturers are free to set initial prices
**Regulatory Limit:**
* Maximum increase allowed:
* 10% per annum
**Compliance Requirements:**
* Any increase beyond 10%:
* Subject to NPPA scrutiny/intervention
---
### 7. Fixation of Ceiling Price for Combination Drugs
* Applies to:
* Fixed Dose Combinations (FDCs)
**Guidelines:**

--- PAGE 8 ---

* Price determined based on:
* Individual component prices
* NPPA may fix separate ceiling price
---
### 8. Local Taxes (Para 4 Explanation)
* Ceiling price is exclusive of local taxes
**Compliance Requirement:**
* Taxes may be added only if:
* Actually paid or payable
---
### 9. Price to Retailer (PTR) Concept
* PTR is the base for ceiling price calculation
**Includes:**
* Manufacturer price
* Wholesaler margin
**Excludes:**
* Retailer margin (added separately)
---
### 10. Mandatory Compliance Summary
* Do not exceed NPPA ceiling price
* Obtain approval for new drug pricing
* Follow WPI-based annual revisions
* Limit non-scheduled drug price increase to 10%
* Apply correct pricing formula and margins
* Ensure accurate tax application
---
### 11. Enforcement Implications
* Any violation (overpricing):
* Leads to recovery of overcharged amount
* Includes interest penalty
* NPPA has authority to:
* Audit pricing data
* Enforce corrective measures
---
### 12. Practical Implementation Guidelines
* Continuously track NPPA price notifications
* Maintain updated pricing databases
* Integrate pricing rules into ERP systems

--- PAGE 9 ---

* Conduct periodic compliance checks
* Document all pricing decisions for audit
---
### 13. Conclusion
The key pricing regulations under DPCO 2013 ensure:
* Uniform pricing of essential medicines
* Prevention of excessive pricing
* Transparent and market-linked pricing mechanism
Strict adherence is mandatory for all pharmaceutical manufacturers and
marketers.

* No drug shall be sold above the notified price

* Update prices immediately after NPPA notifications
