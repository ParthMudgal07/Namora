v**CDSCO COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK
(Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 – Structured Summary)**
---
### 1. Objective of the Act
The Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 regulates the import, manufacture,
distribution, and sale of drugs and cosmetics in India to ensure their
safety, efficacy, and quality.
---
### 2. Regulatory Authority
* Central Drugs Standard Control Organization (CDSCO)
* Headed by the Drugs Controller General of India (DCGI)
* State Drug Control Authorities assist in enforcement
---
### 3. Key Definitions
* **Drug**: Includes medicines for diagnosis, treatment, mitigation, or
prevention of diseases, and substances affecting body structure or function.
* **Cosmetic**: Articles applied to the human body for cleansing,
beautifying, or altering appearance.
* **Manufacture**: Includes any process for making, altering, packaging,
labeling, or repacking drugs.
---
### 4. Import Regulations
* Import of drugs requires a valid import license.
* Drugs must meet prescribed quality standards.
* Misbranded, adulterated, or spurious drugs are prohibited from import.
---
### 5. Manufacturing Regulations
* Manufacturing requires a valid license issued by state authorities.
* Compliance with Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) is mandatory.
* Premises and equipment must meet prescribed standards.
---
### 6. Sale and Distribution
* Sale of drugs requires appropriate licenses (retail/wholesale).
* Certain drugs can only be sold on prescription.
* Records of sale and distribution must be maintained.
---
### 7. Classification of Drugs (Legal Categories)
* **Misbranded Drugs**: Incorrect or misleading labeling.
* **Adulterated Drugs**: Contaminated or prepared under unhygienic
conditions.
* **Spurious Drugs**: Fake or counterfeit drugs.
* These categories are strictly prohibited and punishable.

--- PAGE 2 ---

---
### 8. Labeling and Packaging Requirements
* Must include:
* Drug name
* Manufacturer details
* Batch number
* Expiry date
* False or misleading claims are prohibited.
---
### 9. Quality Control and Testing
* Drugs must comply with standards defined in official pharmacopoeia.
* Government analysts test samples for compliance.
* Inspectors have authority to collect samples.
---
### 10. Inspection and Enforcement
* Drug Inspectors are appointed to:
* Inspect premises
* Take samples
* Investigate violations
* Non-compliance can lead to suspension or cancellation of licenses.
---
### 11. Penalties and Offences
* Manufacturing or selling spurious/adulterated drugs:
* Severe penalties including imprisonment and fines
* Repeat offences attract stricter punishment
---
### 12. Clinical Trials and Approvals (High-Level)
* New drugs require approval before marketing
* Clinical trials must follow regulatory approval and ethical standards
---
### 13. Record Keeping and Documentation
* Mandatory maintenance of:
* Manufacturing records
* Batch records
* Distribution logs
* Records must be available for inspection
---
### 14. Prohibited Activities
* Sale of unlicensed drugs
* False advertisements

--- PAGE 3 ---

* Distribution of unsafe or substandard drugs
---
### 15. Compliance Requirements Summary
To remain compliant, an organization must:
* Obtain necessary licenses (import, manufacture, sale)
* Follow GMP standards
* Ensure accurate labeling
* Maintain proper documentation
* Allow inspections and cooperate with authorities
* Avoid distribution of substandard or illegal drugs
---
**Conclusion**
The Act establishes a strict regulatory framework to ensure that all drugs
and cosmetics in India are safe, effective, and of standard quality. Non-
compliance can result in severe legal consequences, including cancellation of
licenses, fines, and imprisonment.

**CDSCO OPERATIONAL COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK
(Drugs and Cosmetics Rules, 1945 – Structured Summary)**
---
### 1. Licensing Requirements
**1.1 Manufacturing License**
* Mandatory for manufacturing drugs and cosmetics
* Issued by State Licensing Authority
* Requires:
* Qualified technical staff
* Adequate premises and equipment
* Compliance with GMP (Schedule M)
**1.2 Sale License**
* Required for retail and wholesale drug sale
* Types:
* Retail License (Form 20, 21)
* Wholesale License (Form 20B, 21B)
**1.3 Loan License**
* Allows manufacturing using another licensed facility
**1.4 Import License**
* Mandatory for importing drugs into India
* Requires registration certificate and approval from CDSCO

--- PAGE 4 ---

---
### 2. Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) – Schedule M
* Premises must be hygienic and well-maintained
* Separate areas for:
* Production
* Quality control
* Storage
* Proper ventilation, lighting, and sanitation required
* Equipment must be validated and calibrated
* Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) must be followed
---
### 3. Drug Classification and Schedules
**Schedule H**
* Prescription drugs (cannot be sold without prescription)
**Schedule H1**
* Strict record-keeping required
**Schedule X**
* Narcotic and psychotropic substances (high control)
**Schedule G**
* Requires caution labeling
**Schedule M**
* GMP requirements
**Schedule Y**
* Clinical trials and new drug approval
---
### 4. Labeling and Packaging Rules (Rule 96 & 97)
Labels must contain:
* Name of the drug
* Active ingredients
* Batch number
* Manufacturing and expiry date
* Manufacturer details
* Schedule-specific warnings (e.g., “Rx only”)
Prohibited:
* Misleading claims
* False therapeutic claims
---
### 5. Storage and Handling

--- PAGE 5 ---

* Drugs must be stored as per specified conditions:
* Temperature-controlled storage (cold chain where required)
* Protection from contamination
* Expired drugs must be segregated and not sold
---
### 6. Record Keeping
Mandatory records include:
* Batch manufacturing records
* Raw material records
* Distribution logs
* Prescription records (for Schedule H1 and X drugs)
Records must be:
* Accurate
* Up-to-date
* Available for inspection
---
### 7. Quality Control and Testing
* Each batch must be tested before release
* Quality Control (QC) unit must be independent
* Use of approved pharmacopoeia standards
* Retention samples must be maintained
---
### 8. Inspection and Compliance Monitoring
Drug Inspectors are authorized to:
* Inspect premises
* Review records
* Take samples
* Investigate complaints
Non-compliance actions:
* Warning
* Suspension of license
* Cancellation of license
* Legal prosecution
---
### 9. Import and Registration (Schedule D & Related Rules)
* Imported drugs must:
* Be registered with CDSCO
* Meet Indian standards
* Documentation required:
* Certificate of Pharmaceutical Product (CPP)
* Free Sale Certificate
* Manufacturing details

--- PAGE 6 ---

---
### 10. Clinical Trials and New Drugs (Schedule Y)
* Approval required before conducting trials
* Ethics Committee approval mandatory
* Informed consent required from participants
* Adverse event reporting is compulsory
---
### 11. Sale Restrictions
* No sale without valid license
* Prescription drugs sold only against valid prescription
* Online sale regulated and monitored
---
### 12. Recall and Pharmacovigilance
* Mechanism must exist for:
* Drug recall
* Reporting adverse drug reactions (ADR)
* Manufacturers must notify authorities of defects
---
### 13. Personnel Requirements
* Qualified pharmacists required for sale
* Competent technical staff required for manufacturing
* Ongoing training recommended
---
### 14. Prohibited Practices
* Sale of expired drugs
* Manufacture without license
* Distribution of substandard drugs
* Tampering with labels or batch details
---
### 15. Compliance Checklist (Operational View)
An organization must ensure:
* Valid licenses (manufacture/import/sale)
* GMP compliance (Schedule M)
* Proper labeling (Rules 96 & 97)
* Accurate record keeping
* Quality testing of all batches
* Controlled sale of scheduled drugs
* Proper storage conditions
* Inspection readiness at all times
---
**Conclusion**

--- PAGE 7 ---

The Drugs and Cosmetics Rules, 1945 provide detailed operational guidelines
for ensuring drug safety, quality, and regulatory compliance across
manufacturing, sale, distribution, and import activities. These rules form
the backbone of day-to-day compliance under CDSCO.

**CDSCO COSMETICS COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK
(Cosmetics Rules, 2020 – Structured Summary)**
---
### 1. Objective of the Rules
The Cosmetics Rules, 2020 regulate the import, manufacture, labeling, and
sale of cosmetics in India to ensure product safety, quality, and consumer
protection.
---
### 2. Regulatory Authority
* Central Drugs Standard Control Organization (CDSCO)
* State Licensing Authorities (for manufacturing and sale)
* Drugs Controller General of India (DCGI) oversees approvals
---
### 3. Definition of Cosmetic
A cosmetic is any article intended to be applied to the human body for
cleansing, beautifying, promoting attractiveness, or altering appearance
without affecting body structure or functions.
---
### 4. Licensing Requirements
**4.1 Manufacturing License**
* Mandatory for manufacturing cosmetics
* Issued by State Licensing Authority
* Requirements:
* Adequate premises
* Qualified technical staff
* Compliance with Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP)
**4.2 Loan License**
* Allows manufacturing at another licensed facility
**4.3 Import Registration Certificate**
* Mandatory for importing cosmetics into India
* Issued by CDSCO
* Valid for a specified period (typically 5 years)
---
### 5. Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP)
* Clean and hygienic manufacturing environment

--- PAGE 8 ---

* Proper ventilation and lighting
* Equipment must be maintained and calibrated
* Raw materials must be tested before use
* Written Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) required
---
### 6. Labeling Requirements
Each cosmetic product must display:
* Name of the cosmetic
* Name and address of manufacturer
* Country of origin (for imports)
* Batch number
* Manufacturing date and expiry date
* Net contents
* Directions for use (if applicable)
* Warnings or cautions (if necessary)
Prohibited:
* Misleading or false claims
* Use of deceptive labeling
---
### 7. Safety and Quality Standards
* Cosmetics must be safe for intended use
* Must comply with standards prescribed under BIS (Bureau of Indian
Standards)
* No harmful or prohibited ingredients allowed
* Heavy metals and contaminants must be within permissible limits
---
### 8. Prohibited and Restricted Ingredients
* Certain substances are banned or restricted
* Use of unsafe dyes, colors, or preservatives is prohibited
* Ingredients must comply with approved standards
---
### 9. Import Regulations
* Cosmetics must be registered before import
* Required documents:
* Product details
* Ingredient list
* Manufacturing information
* Products must meet Indian safety standards
---
### 10. Testing and Quality Control
* Products must undergo quality testing
* Authorities may:
* Collect samples
* Conduct laboratory testing

--- PAGE 9 ---

* Non-compliant products may be seized or banned
---
### 11. Inspection and Enforcement
Authorities may:
* Inspect manufacturing facilities
* Verify compliance with GMP
* Examine records and documents
Actions for non-compliance:
* Suspension or cancellation of license
* Seizure of products
* Legal penalties
---
### 12. Recall Mechanism
* Manufacturers/importers must:
* Maintain a system for product recall
* Inform authorities in case of safety issues
* Defective products must be withdrawn from the market
---
### 13. Record Keeping
Mandatory records include:
* Manufacturing records
* Batch details
* Raw material records
* Distribution records
Records must be:
* Maintained properly
* Available for inspection
---
### 14. Sale and Distribution
* Sale allowed only through licensed entities
* Products must be properly labeled and safe
* Expired or unsafe cosmetics cannot be sold
---
### 15. Packaging Requirements
* Packaging must protect product integrity
* Must not react with product contents
* Tamper-proof packaging recommended
---
### 16. Compliance Checklist (Operational View)

--- PAGE 10 ---

To ensure compliance, an organization must:
* Obtain valid manufacturing/import license
* Follow GMP standards
* Ensure accurate labeling
* Avoid prohibited ingredients
* Maintain proper records
* Implement recall system
* Ensure product safety and quality
* Be inspection-ready at all times
---
**Conclusion**
The Cosmetics Rules, 2020 establish a comprehensive regulatory framework
ensuring that cosmetic products in India are safe, properly labeled, and
manufactured under controlled conditions. Compliance is mandatory for market
access and consumer safety.

**CDSCO GUIDANCE DOCUMENTS FRAMEWORK
(Practical Implementation & Procedural Compliance Summary)**
---
### 1. Objective of Guidance Documents
CDSCO Guidance Documents provide step-by-step procedures for regulatory
compliance, including application submission, licensing, registration, and
approval processes.
They are intended to:
* Simplify regulatory procedures
* Ensure uniform implementation
* Assist applicants in meeting compliance requirements
---
### 2. Online Regulatory System (SUGAM Portal)
* All applications must be submitted via the CDSCO SUGAM portal
* Applicants must:
* Register on the portal
* Upload required documents
* Track application status online
Key features:
* Digital submission of forms
* Real-time status tracking
* Communication with regulators
---

**3.2 Evaluation Process**
* Document verification by CDSCO

### 4. Manufacturing License Guidance
* Application submitted to State Licensing Authority
* Required documents:
* Site master file
* Plant layout
* Equipment details
* Technical staff qualifications
* Inspection conducted before approval
* Compliance with GMP mandatory
---
### 5. Cosmetics Registration Guidance
* Registration required before import of cosmetics
* Documents required:
* Ingredient list
* Product specifications
* Label details
* Safety data
* Unique registration number issued for each product
---
### 6. Document Submission Standards
All submissions must:
* Be complete and accurate
* Be in prescribed formats
* Include properly signed and stamped documents
* Be in English (or translated)
Incomplete applications may be rejected
---
### 7. Query and Deficiency Handling
* CDSCO may raise queries during evaluation

* Applicants must:
* Respond within specified timelines
* Provide additional documents or clarification
Failure to respond may lead to rejection
---
### 8. Timelines and Processing
* Applications are processed within defined timelines (subject to
completeness)
* Delays occur if:
* Documents are incomplete
* Clarifications are pending
---
### 9. Approval and Certification
Upon successful evaluation:
* Registration Certificate / License is issued

---

After approval, the applicant must:
* Maintain product quality
* Follow labeling and packaging rules
* Report adverse events (if applicable)
* Inform CDSCO about changes (manufacturing, composition, etc.)

### 11. Record Maintenance
Applicants must maintain:
* Application records
* Approval certificates
* Product and batch data
* Communication with authorities
Records must be:
* Updated regularly
* Available for inspection
---
### 12. Inspection and Verification
Authorities may:
* Verify submitted data

* Incomplete documentation
* Incorrect or inconsistent data
* Non-compliance with standards
* Failure to respond to queries
---
### 14. Best Practices for Compliance
* Ensure document accuracy before submission
* Maintain digital and physical records
* Follow prescribed formats strictly
* Monitor application status regularly
* Respond promptly to regulatory queries
---
### 15. Operational Compliance Checklist
To ensure smooth approval:
* Register on SUGAM portal
* Submit complete application with required documents
* Ensure GMP compliance
* Maintain proper documentation
* Track and respond to queries
* Ensure post-approval compliance
---
**Conclusion**
CDSCO Guidance Documents act as procedural manuals that enable organizations
to navigate regulatory requirements efficiently. While not legally binding
like Acts or Rules, they are essential for successful application processing
and regulatory compliance.
